## Required Aircraft Documents (AROW)

### A - Airworthiness Certificate

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.203(a)(1)

**Requirement**: No person may operate a civil aircraft unless it has within it the following:
- A current airworthiness certificate

**Display Requirement**: The airworthiness certificate must be displayed at the cabin or cockpit entrance so that it is legible to passengers or crew.

**Validity**: A Standard Airworthiness Certificate remains valid as long as:
1. The aircraft is maintained in accordance with FAR Part 43
2. The aircraft is properly registered in the United States
3. Required inspections are performed and current

**Categories**:
- **Standard**: Most common, for normal, utility, acrobatic, commuter, and transport category aircraft
- **Limited**: Restricted operations (agricultural, patrol, weather control, etc.)
- **Experimental**: Research, development, amateur-built, exhibition, etc.

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty up to $15,625 per violation (2025 rates per 49 USC § 46301)
- Certificate action against pilot certificate (suspension or revocation per 14 CFR § 91.13)

---

### R - Registration Certificate

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.203(a)(2)

**Requirement**: An effective U.S. registration certificate issued to its owner or, for operation within the United States, the second copy of the Aircraft Registration Application as provided for in § 47.31(c).

**Validity**: 7 years from date of issue (per 14 CFR § 47.40)

**Renewal Process**:
- Renew online at https://registry.faa.gov
- Processing time: 4-6 weeks
- Fee: $5
- Must be renewed every 7 years

**Temporary Registration**:
- Valid for 120 days when new registration pending
- Pink copy of application (AC Form 8050-1) serves as temporary certificate

**Location**: Must be carried aboard aircraft at all times

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Operating with expired registration: Civil penalty up to $15,625
- Aircraft may be deregistered and prohibited from flight

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 47.31 - Aircraft not previously registered
- 14 CFR § 47.40 - Registration expiration and renewal
- 49 USC § 44101-44104 - Aircraft registration requirements

---

### O - Operating Limitations

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.9, Part 21 § 21.5

**Requirement**: Pilot's Operating Handbook (POH) or FAA-Approved Airplane Flight Manual (AFM)

**Specific Requirements**:
- All aircraft produced after March 1, 1979 must have a current and approved flight manual (per Part 21 § 21.5)
- Operating limitations in the form of placards, markings, and instrument range markings
- Equipment list (when required)

**Must Include**:
- Performance limitations
- Normal, abnormal, and emergency procedures
- Fuel, oil, and coolant requirements
- Weight and CG limits
- System descriptions
- Loading instructions

**Location**: Must be accessible to the crew during flight

**Penalties**:
- Operating without required manuals: Civil penalty and/or certificate action
- Liability exposure if accident occurs without proper documentation

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 21.5 - Airplane or Rotorcraft Flight Manual
- 14 CFR § 91.9 - Civil aircraft flight manual, marking, and placard requirements

---

### W - Weight and Balance

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.9(a)

**Requirement**: Current weight and balance data

**Required Information**:
- Empty weight
- Center of gravity (CG) location
- Equipment list showing all installed equipment
- Loading instructions
- CG envelope diagram

**Location**: Must be carried aboard aircraft

**Pilot Responsibility**: While not required to perform W&B calculation for each flight, pilot must ensure aircraft is within limits before flight (14 CFR § 91.7)

**Update Required When**:
- Major alterations or repairs
- Equipment added or removed
- Paint or interior modifications
- Annual inspection (recommended)

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Operating outside weight/CG limits: Civil penalty up to $15,625
- Certificate action (suspension or revocation)
- Possible criminal charges if willful violation
- Insurance may be voided

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 91.9 - Flight manual, marking, and placard requirements
- 14 CFR § 91.7 - Civil aircraft airworthiness
- 14 CFR § 23.25 - Weight limits (for Part 23 aircraft)

## Inspections & Maintenance

### Annual Inspection

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.409(a)(1)

**Interval**: Every 12 calendar months

**Requirement**: Except as provided in paragraph (c) of this section, no person may operate an aircraft unless, within the preceding 12 calendar months, it has had an annual inspection in accordance with part 43 of this chapter.

**Calendar Month Rule**: If the annual inspection is completed in the first 15 days of a month, it may be considered due the last day of the previous month (per 14 CFR § 43.17).

**Example**: Annual completed June 14, 2025 → Next due May 31, 2026

**Who Can Perform**:
- Certificated A&P mechanic with Inspection Authorization (IA)
- FAA-certificated repair station rated for airframe inspections
- Aircraft manufacturer

**Scope (per 14 CFR Part 43 Appendix D)**:
- Complete airframe inspection
- Engine and propeller inspection
- All aircraft systems
- Compliance with Airworthiness Directives
- Control surfaces and rigging
- Fuel and oil systems
- Landing gear
- Electrical system
- Emergency equipment

**Required Logbook Entry (per 14 CFR § 43.11)**:
- Type of inspection performed
- Date of inspection
- Aircraft total time in service
- Signature, certificate number, and kind of certificate held
- Statement that aircraft is approved or disapproved for return to service

**Estimated Cost**: $1,200-$2,500 (single-engine piston, varies by region and discrepancies)

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty: $5,000-$15,625 per violation
- Certificate action against pilot and/or owner
- Aircraft not airworthy and may not be flown

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 91.409(a) - Annual inspection requirement
- 14 CFR Part 43 Appendix D - Scope and detail of items to include
- 14 CFR § 43.11 - Content, form, and disposition of inspection records
- 14 CFR § 43.15 - Additional performance rules for inspections

### 100-Hour Inspection

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.409(b)

**Interval**: Every 100 hours of tachometer time

**Requirement**: No person may operate an aircraft carrying any person (other than a crewmember) for hire, and no person may give flight instruction for hire in an aircraft which that person provides, unless within the preceding 100 hours of time in service the aircraft has received an annual or 100-hour inspection.

**When Required**:
1. Aircraft carrying any person for hire
2. Flight instruction when both aircraft AND instructor provided by same person/entity

**Exception - 10-Hour Extension**: The 100-hour limitation may be exceeded by not more than 10 hours while en route to reach a place where the inspection can be done. The excess time used to reach the inspection location must be included in computing the next 100 hours of time in service (14 CFR § 91.409(b)).

**Example**:
- 100-hour due at 1200.0 tach
- Fly 8 hours to reach maintenance facility → Total: 1208.0
- Next 100-hour due: 1208.0 (not 1300.0!)

**Time Tracking**: Based on **tachometer time**, not Hobbs meter

**Tach vs. Hobbs**:
- **Tachometer**: RPM-based, used for maintenance intervals
- **Hobbs**: Continuous elapsed time, used for billing/logging

**Who Can Perform**:
- Certificated A&P mechanic (IA not required)
- FAA-certificated repair station
- Aircraft manufacturer

**Scope**: Similar to annual inspection in depth and detail (Part 43 Appendix D)

**100-Hour vs. Annual**:
- 100-hour inspection does NOT substitute for annual inspection
- Annual inspection DOES satisfy 100-hour requirement

**Estimated Cost**: $1,200-$2,500 (similar to annual)

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty: $5,000-$15,625
- Certificate action against pilot and operator
- Violation of Part 135 or Part 141 operations specifications

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 91.409(b) - 100-hour inspection requirement
- 14 CFR § 91.409(e) - 10-hour exception
- 14 CFR Part 43 Appendix D - Scope and detail

### Transponder Inspection

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.413

**Interval**: Every 24 calendar months

**Full Text**: "No persons may use an ATC transponder that is specified in § 91.215(a), § 121.345(c), or § 135.143(c) unless, within the preceding 24 calendar months, the ATC transponder has been tested and inspected and found to comply with appendix F of part 43."

**When Required**:
- All aircraft operating in Mode C airspace:
  - Class B airspace
  - Class C airspace
  - Within 30 nautical miles of Class B primary airport (Mode C veil)
- Aircraft operating in Class A airspace (above 18,000 ft MSL)
- Required for ADS-B Out compliance

**Who Can Perform**:
- Aircraft manufacturer
- FAA-certificated repair station with radio rating
- Certificated mechanic with appropriate ratings (limited scope)

**Tests Required (14 CFR Part 43 Appendix F)**:
1. Radio Reply Frequency (1030 MHz interrogation, 1090 MHz reply)
2. Suppression
3. Receiver Sensitivity
4. Mode C (automatic altitude reporting)
5. Mode S (if equipped)
6. ADS-B Out (if equipped)

**Integrated System Test**: When transponder maintenance could introduce data correspondence error between automatic pressure altitude and displayed altitude:
- Automatic pressure altitude at output measured
- Difference between transponder altitude and displayed altitude must not exceed **125 feet** (per 14 CFR § 91.217)

**Required Logbook Entry**:
- Date of inspection
- Aircraft total time in service
- Description of work performed per Appendix F
- Signature, certificate number, and kind of certificate
- Statement that aircraft approved/disapproved for return to service

**Estimated Cost**:
- Transponder only: $150-$250
- Bundled with altimeter/static: $350-$500

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty: $2,500-$15,625
- May be denied clearance into Class B/C airspace
- ATC may report violation
- Certificate action possible

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 91.413 - ATC transponder tests and inspections
- 14 CFR Part 43 Appendix F - ATC Transponder Tests and Inspections
- 14 CFR § 91.215 - ATC transponder and altitude reporting equipment requirements
- 14 CFR § 91.217 - Data correspondence between automatically reported pressure altitude

### Altimeter and Static System Inspection

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.411

**Interval**: Every 24 calendar months

**Full Text**: "No person may operate an airplane, or helicopter, in controlled airspace under IFR unless—Within the preceding 24 calendar months, each static pressure system, each altimeter instrument, and each automatic pressure altitude reporting system has been tested and inspected and found to comply with appendixes E and F of part 43."

**When Required**:
- All aircraft operated in controlled airspace under IFR
- Required even if aircraft only occasionally flies IFR

**Who Can Perform**:
- Aircraft manufacturer
- FAA-certificated repair station properly equipped for these functions
- Certificated mechanic with airframe rating (static pressure system tests and inspections only per 14 CFR § 43.3(d))

**Tests Required (14 CFR Part 43 Appendix E)**:

**1. Static System Leak Test**:
- System must hold pressure within specified tolerances
- Test at equivalent altitude of maximum aircraft operating altitude

**2. Altimeter Test**:
- Tested at multiple pressure altitudes
- Test points: -1,000; 0; 500; 1,000; 1,500; 2,000; 3,000; 4,000; 6,000; 8,000; 10,000; 15,000; 20,000; 25,000; 30,000 feet
- Tolerances specified in Appendix E Table I

**3. Altitude Reporting Equipment**:
- Mode C integration test
- Automatic reporting must correlate with altimeter within 125 feet

**4. Static System Integrity**:
- Check for leaks, damage, blockage
- Alternate static source (if installed)

**Bundling**: This inspection (91.411) and transponder inspection (91.413) are commonly performed together for cost savings

**Required Logbook Entry**:
- Date and aircraft total time in service
- Description of tests performed
- Results of tests
- Signature, certificate number, kind of certificate
- Statement that aircraft approved/disapproved for return to service

**Estimated Cost**:
- Altimeter/Static only: $200-$300
- Bundled with transponder: $350-$500

**VFR-Only Aircraft**: If aircraft will never operate IFR, this inspection is not required. However, many owners maintain compliance for resale value and operational flexibility.

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty: $2,500-$15,625
- Pilot certificate action
- IFR clearance may be denied if ATC aware of non-compliance

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 91.411 - Altimeter system and altitude reporting equipment tests
- 14 CFR Part 43 Appendix E - Altimeter System Test and Inspection
- 14 CFR § 43.3(d) - Who may perform maintenance

### ELT Inspection

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.207(d)

**Interval**: Every 12 calendar months

**Requirement**: "Except as provided in paragraph (f) of this section, no person may operate a U.S. registered civil airplane unless there is attached to the airplane an approved automatic type emergency locator transmitter that is in operable condition."

**Inspection Requirements (per 14 CFR § 91.207(d))**:
1. Proper installation
2. Battery corrosion check
3. Operation of controls and crash sensor
4. Sufficient signal strength on 121.5 MHz

**Battery Replacement Required When** (per 14 CFR § 91.207(c)):
- 50% of useful life has expired
- ELT has been in use for more than 1 cumulative hour
- Manufacturer's specified expiration date reached

**Battery Expiration Marking**: New battery expiration date must be legibly marked on outside of ELT

**406 MHz ELTs**:
- Modern ELTs use 406 MHz (monitored by COSPAS-SARSAT satellite system)
- Must be registered with NOAA: https://beaconregistration.noaa.gov/
- Registration must be updated when:
  - Ownership changes
  - Aircraft registration changes
  - Contact information changes

**Who Can Perform**:
- Certificated mechanic
- Certificated repair station
- Pilot (battery replacement only, if manufacturer allows per 14 CFR § 43 Appendix A(c))

**Required Logbook Entry**:
- Date of inspection
- Condition of battery
- Operation test results
- Signature and certificate number

**Estimated Cost**:
- ELT inspection: $50-$100 (if done with annual)
- Battery replacement: $150-$400 (depending on ELT model)
- New ELT installation: $800-$1,500

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty up to $1,500
- ELT must be operational unless placarded and pilot notified
- Insurance implications if ELT not functional during accident

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 91.207 - Emergency locator transmitters
- TSO-C126 - 406 MHz ELT standards
- 47 CFR § 87.199 - ELT registration requirements

### VOR Check

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.171

**Interval**: Every 30 days

**Full Text**: "No person may operate a civil aircraft under IFR using the VOR system of radio navigation unless the VOR equipment of that aircraft—Has been maintained, checked, and inspected under an approved procedure; or—Has been operationally checked within the preceding 30 days, and was found to be within the limits of the permissible indicated bearing error."

**When Required**: Only if VOR equipment is used for IFR navigation

**Exemption**: If aircraft has IFR-certified GPS (TSO-C129/C145/C146) and GPS is primary navigation, VOR check not required for GPS operations (however, if VOR used as backup, check still required)

**Methods of VOR Check (per 14 CFR § 91.171(b))**:

**1. VOT Check** (VOR Test Facility):
- Tolerance: ±4 degrees
- Most accurate method
- Tune VOR to VOT frequency
- Should indicate 360° TO or 180° FROM

**2. Ground Checkpoint**:
- Published ground checkpoint on airport
- Tolerance: ±4 degrees
- Listed in Chart Supplement (formerly Airport/Facility Directory)

**3. Airborne Checkpoint**:
- Published airborne checkpoint
- Tolerance: ±6 degrees
- Listed in Chart Supplement

**4. Dual VOR Cross-Check**:
- Both VORs tuned to same station
- Difference must not exceed 4 degrees
- Can be done in flight

**5. Selected Ground Reference Point**:
- If no checkpoints available
- Select prominent ground reference on published radial
- Tolerance: ±6 degrees

**Required Entry (14 CFR § 91.171(d))** - Must be entered in aircraft logbook or other record:
- Date
- Place
- Bearing error
- Signature

**Example Entry**: "10/01/2025 - VOT check at KMCO - +2° - John Smith"

**Penalties**:
- Using VOR for IFR with expired check: Civil penalty $1,000-$5,000
- Pilot certificate action
- Not legal to file IFR if using VOR without current check

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 91.171 - VOR equipment check for IFR operations
- Chart Supplement - VOT locations and checkpoints

### Airworthiness Directives (ADs)

**Regulation**: 14 CFR Part 39

**Authority**: 49 USC § 44701(a)(5) - FAA authority to issue ADs

**Requirement (14 CFR § 39.3)**: "An airworthiness directive is a legally enforceable rule that applies to...aircraft, aircraft engines, propellers, or appliances..."

**Legal Effect (14 CFR § 39.7)**: "Anyone who operates a product that does not meet the requirements of an applicable airworthiness directive is in violation of this section."

**Owner Responsibility (14 CFR § 91.403(c))**: "Each owner or operator shall ensure that maintenance personnel make appropriate entries in the aircraft maintenance records indicating the aircraft has been approved for return to service."

**Types of ADs**:

**1. One-Time ADs**:
- Single inspection or action required
- Example: Inspect wing spar for cracks (one-time)

**2. Recurring ADs**:
- Repeated inspections or actions at specified intervals
- Can be calendar-based or hour-based
- Example: Inspect control cables every 100 flight hours

**3. Emergency ADs**:
- Issued when immediate safety concern discovered
- May ground aircraft immediately until compliance

**AD Applicability**:
- Airframe (by aircraft make/model)
- Engine (by engine make/model)
- Propeller (by propeller make/model)
- Appliances (instruments, systems, components)

**AD Research**:
- FAA AD Database: https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/airworthiness_directives
- Search by make and model
- Review AD notes for specific serial number applicability

**Required Compliance Record**:
Each AD must have logbook entry showing:
- AD number
- Date of compliance
- Method of compliance
- Recurring requirements (if any)
- Signature and certificate number

**Example Entry**:
```
AD 2023-15-06 complied with on 06/15/2025 at 3,450.2 TT.
Wing spar inspection performed per AD instructions. No cracks found.
Recurring inspection required every 100 flight hours.
Next due: 3,550.2 TT (approx. 12/01/2025).
John Smith, A&P #1234567890
```

**Alternative Methods of Compliance (AMOC)** (14 CFR § 39.19):
- May request FAA approval for alternative compliance method
- Must be approved in writing before implementation
- Submit request to FAA with justification

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty: $7,500-$15,625 per AD
- Aircraft not airworthy
- Certificate action against pilot and owner
- Criminal penalties for willful violations (49 USC § 46316)
- Liability exposure if accident related to non-compliant AD

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR Part 39 - Airworthiness Directives
- 14 CFR § 91.403(c) - Owner responsibility
- 49 USC § 44701 - FAA authority
- 49 USC § 46316 - Criminal penalties

## International Operations

### CBP Customs Decal

**Regulation**: 19 CFR § 122.22 - Persons, carriers, and merchandise arriving by aircraft

**Authority**: Customs User Fees under Consolidated Omnibus Budget Reconciliation Act (COBRA)

**Requirement**: All private aircraft entering the United States from international destinations must have a valid CBP annual user fee decal OR pay per-arrival fee

**Purchase Process**:
- Decal Transponder Online Procurement System (DTOPS): https://dtops.cbp.dhs.gov/
- Valid for calendar year (January 1 - December 31)
- Must be affixed to aircraft in visible location

**Fees (2025 - Subject to Annual COBRA Adjustment)**:
- Annual Decal: $31.00
- Per-arrival fee (without decal): $27.50 per entry

**Contact Information**:
- User Fee Help Desk: (317) 298-1245
- Email: decals@cbp.dhs.gov

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Per-arrival fee charged ($27.50 each entry)
- Potential delays in customs processing
- Civil penalties for repeated non-compliance

**Citations**:
- 19 CFR § 122.22 - Aircraft arrivals
- 19 CFR § 24.22 - User fees for aircraft
- Federal Register 2024-15990 - FY 2025 user fee adjustments

### eAPIS (Electronic Advance Passenger Information System)

**Regulation**: 19 CFR § 122.49a

**Authority**: Aviation and Transportation Security Act (ATSA)

**Requirement**: All aircraft on international flights arriving to or departing from the United States must provide passenger/crew manifest

**Enrollment**:
- Online system: https://eapis.cbp.dhs.gov/
- First-time users must enroll (no fee)
- Account required for each aircraft operator

**Submission Requirements**:

**For Arrivals**:
- Submit no later than **30 minutes prior to arrival** to United States
- Includes all passengers, crew members, and non-crew members

**For Departures**:
- Submit minimum **1 hour prior to departure** (no maximum timeframe)
- Must transmit no later than **30 minutes after departure** from United States

**Required Information**:
- Full name (as shown on passport)
- Date of birth
- Citizenship
- Passport number and country of issuance
- Address while in US (arrivals) or foreign address (departures)
- Aircraft registration number
- Departure and arrival airports (ICAO codes)
- Estimated departure/arrival times

**Responsible Party**: Pilot-in-command or authorized designee

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty up to $5,000 per violation (per 8 USC § 1221(c))
- Criminal penalties for willful violations (49 USC § 46311)
- Aircraft may be denied entry or departure clearance
- Pilot certificate action possible

**Support Contact**: GAsupport@cbp.dhs.gov

**Citations**:
- 19 CFR § 122.49a - Electronic manifest requirement for all arriving aircraft
- 8 USC § 1221(c) - Penalties
- 49 USC § 46311 - Criminal penalties

### FCC Radio Station License

**Regulation**: 47 CFR Part 87 - Aviation Services

**Requirement**: Aircraft making international flights must have FCC Radio Station License

**Exemption**: Aircraft operating **domestically only** within United States do NOT require license (exemption granted December 12, 1996 per FCC Public Notice)

**License Details**:
- **Validity**: 10 years
- **Application**: FCC Form 605
- **Fee**: Approximately $170 (subject to change)
- **Callsign**: FCC assigns unique callsign separate from FAA N-number
- **Application URL**: https://www.fcc.gov/aircraft-stations

**Callsign Usage**: Operators should identify transmissions with FCC callsign or FAA "N" number during international operations

**Renewal**:
- Must be renewed every 10 years
- Apply through FCC Universal Licensing System (ULS)
- Processing time: 7-10 business days

**ICAO Requirement**: ICAO Annex 10 specifically states radio station license required if aircraft equipped with radios on international flights

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil forfeiture up to $10,000 per violation (47 USC § 503(b))
- Additional criminal penalties for willful violations (47 USC § 501)
- May be denied clearance for international operations

**Citations**:
- 47 CFR Part 87 - Aviation Services
- 47 CFR § 87.18 - Station license required
- 47 USC § 301 - License requirement
- 47 USC § 503(b) - Forfeitures

### Restricted Radiotelephone Operator Permit

**Regulation**: 47 CFR § 13.3

**Requirement**: At least one person aboard aircraft on international flights must hold Restricted Radiotelephone Operator Permit

**Permit Details**:
- **Validity**: Lifetime (no expiration)
- **Exam**: None required
- **Fee**: Approximately $65
- **Application**: FCC Form 605

**Application Process**:
- Online through FCC Universal Licensing System (ULS)
- Can use third-party services (Sporty's Academy, etc.) for convenience
- Processing time: 2-3 weeks

**Who Needs It**: Pilot or any crew member on international flights (required in addition to aircraft radio station license)

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil forfeiture up to $10,000 per violation
- May be denied clearance for international operations

**Citations**:
- 47 CFR § 13.3 - Commercial radio operator licenses
- 47 CFR § 13.7 - Restricted Radiotelephone Operator Permit
- 47 USC § 303(l) - Operator license requirement

## Operation-Specific Requirements

### Part 91 - General Operations (Flying Clubs)

**Primary Regulation**: 14 CFR Part 91

**Inspection Requirements**:
- **Annual Inspection**: Required (14 CFR § 91.409(a))
- **100-Hour Inspection**: Only if aircraft used for compensation or hire
- **Transponder**: Required if operating in controlled airspace (24 months)
- **Altimeter/Static**: Required if operating IFR (24 months)
- **ELT**: Required (12 months)
- **VOR Check**: Only if using VOR for IFR (30 days)
- **Airworthiness Directives**: All applicable ADs must be complied with

**FAA Oversight**: Minimal compared to commercial operations

**Owner-Performed Maintenance**: Part 91 owner-operator may perform preventive maintenance if holds at least private pilot certificate (per 14 CFR § 43 Appendix A)

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR Part 91 - General Operating and Flight Rules
- 14 CFR § 43 Appendix A - Preventive maintenance

---

### Part 61 - Flight Schools (Standard Training)

**Primary Regulation**: 14 CFR Part 61

**100-Hour Requirement**: Aircraft AND instructor provided by same entity triggers 100-hour inspection requirement

**When NOT Required**:
- Student rents aircraft without instructor
- Independent instructor uses student's aircraft

**Inspection Requirements**:
- **Annual**: Required
- **100-Hour**: Required when providing aircraft + instructor
- **Transponder**: Required (24 months)
- **Altimeter/Static**: Required for IFR training (24 months)
- **ELT**: Required (12 months)
- **VOR Check**: Required for IFR training aircraft (30 days)

**FAA Oversight**: Limited oversight - no FAA approval required to operate as Part 61 school

**High Utilization Impact**:
- Aircraft flying 500-800 hours/year = 5-8 x 100-hour inspections annually
- Annual cost: $6,000-$20,000 per aircraft for inspections alone

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR Part 61 - Certification: Pilots, Flight Instructors, and Ground Instructors
- 14 CFR § 91.409(b) - 100-hour inspection for hire

---

### Part 141 - Flight Schools (FAA-Approved Programs)

**Primary Regulation**: 14 CFR Part 141

**Additional Requirements Beyond Part 61**:
- FAA-approved Training Course Outline (TCO)
- Chief instructor designated and qualified
- Quality control procedures required
- Regular FAA conformity checks (unannounced)

**Aircraft Maintenance Standards**:
- Must be maintained per Part 91 Subpart E OR equivalent foreign aviation authority standards
- Higher standard of maintenance expected
- FAA maintenance inspectors perform conformity checks

**Part 141 Certificate Required**:
- School must apply for and maintain Part 141 certificate
- Annual FAA inspections of school operations and aircraft
- Must have adequate facilities and equipment

**Advantages**:
- Reduced flight time requirements for certificates/ratings
- Structured training recognized by airlines and military
- Eligible for VA benefits and international student visas

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR Part 141 - Pilot Schools
- 14 CFR § 141.39 - Aircraft requirements
- 14 CFR § 141.91 - Satellite bases

### Part 135 - Charter Operations (Air Taxi/Commuter)

**Primary Regulation**: 14 CFR Part 135

**Aircraft Seating Configuration Determines Requirements**:

**9 or Fewer Passenger Seats**:
- Annual OR 100-hour OR FAA-approved Aircraft Inspection Program
- Maintained under Parts 91 and 43 plus §135.415, §135.417, §135.421, §135.422
- Manufacturer TBO compliance **required**

**10 or More Passenger Seats**:
- **Continuous Airworthiness Maintenance Program (CAMP) mandatory**
- Separate General Maintenance Manual required
- Enhanced maintenance program per §135.423-135.443

**Required Documents at Principal Business Office** (14 CFR § 135.63):
- Operating Certificate
- Operations Specifications (OpSpecs)
- Current list of aircraft used in operations
- Pilot records (aeronautical experience, qualifications)
- Current flight manual for each aircraft type

**MEL/CDL Requirements**:

**Minimum Equipment List (MEL)** - OpSpec D095:
- Tail-specific, custom made for each aircraft
- Derived from FAA Master Minimum Equipment List (MMEL)
- Repair categories:
  - **Category A**: Per manufacturer-specified interval
  - **Category B**: 3 days
  - **Category C**: 10 days
  - **Category D**: 120 days

**Configuration Deviation List (CDL)**:
- Addresses external items (fairings, wheel covers, etc.)

**Engine Trend Monitoring** (14 CFR § 135.421):
- Oil analysis at each 100-hour interval OR manufacturer's interval (whichever more frequent)

**Manufacturer TBO Compliance**:
- Part 135 **must** comply with manufacturer Time Between Overhaul limits
- Part 91 operations may operate beyond TBO with proper maintenance

**Maintenance Records** (14 CFR § 135.439):
- Complete documentation required - no gaps allowed
- Records kept at principal business office
- Available for FAA inspection at all times

**FAA Oversight**: Extensive surveillance, regular inspections, Principal Operations Inspector (POI) assigned

**Penalties for Non-Compliance**:
- Civil penalty up to $37,500 per violation (higher than Part 91)
- Certificate action against operating certificate
- Aircraft grounding
- Criminal penalties for willful violations

## Maintenance Records

### Record Entry Requirements

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 43.9 (Maintenance Records) and 14 CFR § 43.11 (Inspection Records)

**Required Information per 14 CFR § 43.9**:
1. Description of work performed (or reference to acceptable data)
2. Date work completed
3. Name of person performing work
4. Signature, certificate number, and kind of certificate held
5. Statement if work not satisfactory: "Not approved for return to service"

**Additional Requirements for Inspections per 14 CFR § 43.11**:
1. Type of inspection performed
2. Brief description of extent of inspection
3. Date of inspection
4. Aircraft total time in service
5. Signature, certificate number, kind of certificate
6. Statement: "Aircraft approved/disapproved for return to service"

**Example Annual Inspection Entry**:
```
Annual inspection performed per FAR 43 Appendix D.
Inspection included complete airframe, engine, and systems inspection.
All Airworthiness Directives reviewed and complied with.
Aircraft approved for return to service.

Date: 06/15/2025
Aircraft Total Time: 3,450.2 hours
Tach Time: 2,105.8 hours

John Smith, A&P IA
Certificate #1234567890
```

---

### Record Retention

**Regulation**: 14 CFR § 91.417

**Permanent Records** (§ 91.417(a)(2)) - Transfer with aircraft at sale:
1. Total time in service of airframe, engine, and propeller
2. Current status of life-limited parts (time remaining)
3. Time since last overhaul of items required to be overhauled on time basis
4. Current inspection status (annual, 100-hour, progressive, etc.)
5. Current status of applicable Airworthiness Directives
6. Copies of FAA Form 337 (major repairs and alterations)

**Temporary Records** (§ 91.417(a)(1)) - Keep 1 year or until superseded:
1. Records of maintenance, preventive maintenance, and alterations
2. Records of 100-hour, annual, progressive, and other required inspections

**Owner/Operator Responsibility** (§ 91.417(c)):
"The owner or operator shall make all maintenance records required to be kept by this section available for inspection by the Administrator or any authorized representative of the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB)."

**Electronic Logbooks**: Acceptable if meet requirements of §§ 43.9, 43.11, and 91.417; must be able to produce in legible form

**Advisory Circular**: AC 43-9C (Change 2) - Maintenance Records

**Citations**:
- 14 CFR § 43.9 - Maintenance record content
- 14 CFR § 43.11 - Inspection record content
- 14 CFR § 43.12 - Falsification of records (criminal offense)
- 14 CFR § 91.417 - Record retention

## Penalties & Enforcement

### Civil Penalty Amounts (2025)

**Authority**: 49 USC § 46301 (adjusted annually for inflation per Federal Register)

| Violation Type | Maximum Penalty |
|----------------|-----------------|
| General aviation violations (Part 91) | $15,625 per violation |
| Commercial operator violations (Part 135) | $37,500 per violation |
| Hazardous materials violations | $96,624 per violation |
| FCC radio violations | $10,000 per violation |
| CBP customs violations | $5,000 per violation |

**Note**: Penalties can be assessed **per flight** or **per day** depending on violation type

**Citation**: Federal Register Doc. 2024-30608 - "Revisions to Civil Penalty Amounts, 2025"

---

### Certificate Actions

**Authority**: 14 CFR § 13 - Investigative and Enforcement Procedures

**Types of Actions**:
1. **Warning Notice** - Informal action, no appeal rights
2. **Letter of Correction** - Educational, not punitive
3. **Certificate Suspension** - Temporary (30-180 days typical)
4. **Certificate Revocation** - Permanent loss of certificate

**Factors Considered** (per FAA Order 2150.3C):
- Nature and severity of violation
- Hazard to air safety
- Whether violation was inadvertent or deliberate
- Prior compliance history
- Corrective action taken
- Attitude of violator

---

### Criminal Penalties

**Authority**: 49 USC § 46316 - General criminal penalty

**When Applied**:
- Willful violations that endanger safety
- Fraud in maintenance records or applications
- Operating unairworthy aircraft knowing it to be unsafe

**Penalties**:
- Fines up to $250,000
- Prison sentences up to 3 years
- Both fines and imprisonment

**Examples**:
- Falsifying maintenance logbooks (49 USC § 46316)
- Operating with known unairworthy condition
- Deliberately ignoring Airworthiness Directives
